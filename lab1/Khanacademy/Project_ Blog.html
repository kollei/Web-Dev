Almaty In Danger

Posted on 27th Jan 2026

Almaty is a city surrounded by mountains, full of greenery and vibrant culture. Yet behind this beauty lies a serious issue: the air is often polluted. Especially in winter, when heating season begins and the wind is too weak to disperse the smog, a heavy gray blanket hangs over the city.
On the streets, you can smell smoke, and visibility drops because of the haze. Residents complain of coughing, fatigue, and headaches — all signs of breathing unhealthy air.
The reasons are clear: too many cars, outdated heating systems, and the city’s geography — sitting in a valley where pollution gets trapped.
More and more people in Almaty are talking about change: using public transport, supporting eco-initiatives, and demanding real solutions from authorities. Because Almaty deserves clean air just as much as any modern city.

LRT in Astana

Posted on 28th Jan 2026

Astana, the capital of Kazakhstan, has long dreamed of modern public transport solutions. One of the most talked-about projects is the LRT (Light Rail Transit). Initially envisioned as a sleek, futuristic system to ease traffic and connect key parts of the city, the project has instead become a symbol of delays and controversy.
Construction started years ago, but progress has been slow. Many residents see unfinished pillars and empty tracks as reminders of wasted potential.
The idea behind the LRT was strong: reduce car dependency, cut pollution, and give Astana a world-class transport option. Yet the reality has been different — funding issues, management problems, and shifting priorities have left the project in limbo.
People in Astana often joke that the LRT is more of a monument than a transport system. Still, there’s hope that one day the project will be revived and finally serve its purpose: moving thousands of passengers efficiently across the capital.
